Case Study

WeMove

Fitness app for safety and community

Role UX Designer & Researcher
Team 4 people
Duration 8 weeks
Tools Figma, Interviews, Testing

The Problem

Many women avoid exercising alone — especially in the evenings — due to safety concerns, lack of motivation, and the absence of a supportive workout community. This leads to decreased physical activity despite the desire to stay active.

Through interviews with seven women aged 20–45, we identified three core challenges:

  • Safety concerns — fear of exercising alone, particularly after dark
  • Motivation — difficult to stay motivated without support
  • Loneliness — missing workout partners with similar goals

Customer Journey

The Solution

We designed an app that combines safety, community, and motivation to help women find workout partners in a secure and supportive way.

Core Features

Safety

  • BankID verification
  • Live GPS sharing
  • SOS button
  • Reporting function

Community

  • Matching based on interests and pace
  • Built-in chat
  • Map view showing nearby users
  • Create groups

Motivation

  • Push notifications
  • Activity logging
  • Shared goals
  • Progress statistics

Prototype & Design

We created a clickable Figma prototype with a focus on safety and user-friendliness.

Map view to start and track workouts

Dashboard with calendar and statistics

Community feed to share activities

User Testing

We tested the prototype with five women aged 22–45, who completed realistic tasks such as creating a profile, finding a workout buddy, starting a workout, and chatting.

What worked

  • BankID verification created a strong sense of safety
  • The SOS button was easy to find and appreciated
  • The map view felt intuitive
  • The design was perceived as warm and welcoming

Areas for improvement

  • The home page needed a clearer introduction
  • The “Start workout” button was difficult to locate
  • Some icons were unclear
  • Users wanted more control over map visibility

My Learnings

1. Safety is the foundation — not a “nice-to-have”

Users’ sense of security shaped every design decision. Without strong safety features, the app would not be viable.

2. Small design choices have big impact

A single unclear button or icon can prevent users from completing key tasks. Testing every detail matters.

3. User testing reveals blind spots

We assumed certain icons were self-explanatory — users interpreted them completely differently. This reinforced the importance of testing early and often.

4. Empathy is at the core of UX

My background in customer service helped me listen deeply and understand not just what users said, but how they felt. This emotional layer guided many design decisions.
